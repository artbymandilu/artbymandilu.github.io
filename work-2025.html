Cloud and Moon Nightlight

I created this night light as a birthday gift for my younger brother. The arched poster board imitates the form of cumulus clouds, while the spherical container (previously packaging for a toy) appears as a moon following the cloud. I created several study models of the clouds (one of which is in the bottom right corner) before creating the final model with LED lights. This model fosters both stunning light and interwoven shadows, creating a simultaneous sense of both comfort and wonder.

Overlap

I created this piece at the CMU Pre-College Architecture Program. It was derived from a pattern by Esmeralda “Es” Devlin, shown on the left. To maintain a consistent language with Devlin’s pattern, I incorporated similar spiraling, geometric shapes, and overlapping colors. Just as the pattern’s texture remains consistent, I used consistent spiraling shapes and materials throughout. However, I used many differing colors, which overlap differently depending on one's viewpoint, to imitate the discontinuities in color across the pattern’s geometric shapes.

Dappled

I created this wearable while attending the Carnegie Mellon University Pre-college program. It incorporates elements from serial sections of Overlap. The 3D model that this wearable was partially derived from inspired me to explore further the materiality of light in how it spills and overlaps. The triangular and translucent colored plastics dapple colored light onto surrounding surfaces when near a light source. The elbow and wrist joints allow for complete mobility, giving the user control over how the dapples of light move and interact
